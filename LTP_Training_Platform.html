Hughes' 16 play types

Bob Hughes identified 16 distinct types of play, each with unique developmental benefits. As a playworker you must be able to identify these in real time. Click any card to ask the AI Tutor for more detail and Ethiopian examples.

The 8 Playwork Principles

These 8 Principles are the philosophical foundation of everything you will learn. Read them, discuss them, make them yours. Click any principle to explore it further.

Critical requirement: You must achieve a minimum score of 80% on the safeguarding knowledge test before you may work unsupervised with children. Child protection is the most important thing you will ever do as a playworker.

Ethiopian cultural play activities
Ethiopian children's play heritage is ancient, diverse, and extraordinarily rich. As a playworker, you are a guardian of living cultural traditions that have supported children's development for thousands of years. Every session should include at least one cultural play element.
The 6 dimensions of play environment quality

The play environment is your most powerful tool. A well-designed environment generates high-quality play with minimal intervention. Rate your site against these 6 dimensions.

Loose Parts Theory — Simon Nicholson (1971)
"In any environment, both the degree of inventiveness and creativity, and the possibility of discovery, are directly proportional to the number and kinds of variables in it."

The more movable, transformable, open-ended materials in a play space, the richer the play. A fixed climbing frame invites one type of play. A collection of planks, ropes, tyres, and crates invites thousands.

Reflective practice journal
Gibbs' Reflective Cycle — required for Level 2 and above
Reflective practice is not a diary. It is a structured process of examining your own professional decisions and values in order to learn and improve. Complete one entry per session in the field.

Description
A 7-year-old girl (Meron) spent 45 minutes arranging stones in patterns alone. Three times I almost intervened to suggest she play with others. Each time I held back.
Analysis
Principle 7 — my presence changes the space. This was Object play, possibly moving into Mastery. She did not need me. The impulse to intervene came from my discomfort, not from her need.
Action plan
Next session: hold back for 5 minutes longer than feels comfortable before considering any response to solo play.
